Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as A.P.J. Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist, engineer, and statesman. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, a small town in Tamil Nadu, India. Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's defense and space programs and served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.


Dr. Kalam hailed from a modest background and his early life was filled with hardships. Despite facing financial constraints, he was a brilliant student with a strong passion for learning. He obtained a degree in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later completed his Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) in Chennai.


After completing his education, Dr. Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist. He contributed significantly to the development of India's missile program, particularly the development of ballistic missiles like Agni and Prithvi. His expertise and leadership skills earned him the position of Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India.


Dr. Kalam's notable achievements in the field of space research include his role as the project director of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in orbit. He also played a crucial role in the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and the Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV).


Apart from his scientific contributions, Dr. Kalam was a visionary leader who emphasized the importance of technology and education in transforming India into a developed nation. He believed in igniting young minds and inspiring them to dream big. Dr. Kalam dedicated a significant part of his life to teaching and interacting with students, particularly encouraging them to pursue scientific research and innovation.


In recognition of his contributions, Dr. Kalam was honored with numerous awards and accolades, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award. He was also a prolific writer and authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which aimed to inspire and motivate the youth of India.


In 2002, Dr. A.P.J. Kalam was elected as the President of India, becoming the first scientist to hold this prestigious position. During his presidency, he continued to advocate for the advancement of science and technology, and he traveled extensively, connecting with people from all walks of life.


On July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Dr. Kalam suffered a cardiac arrest and passed away. His sudden demise was mourned by people across the nation and beyond. Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's legacy lives on as a source of inspiration for countless individuals, and he is fondly remembered as the "People's President" and the "Missile Man of India."
